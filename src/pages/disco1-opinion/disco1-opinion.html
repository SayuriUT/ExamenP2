Anónimo

Enero 20, 2011

 Why is this album so poorly rated? It's a pop rock album that actually made it through the filth of the radio...and it delivers. It's fun, it really touts the influences of the members, and it's not your average pop music by any stretch. And it's great to hear that Adam Levine isn't the only great part, the band work together smoothly to craft unique and funky pop gems that feel like they could be compared to the post grunge and alternative rock of the 90's and the funk, soul and R&B of the 70's. If you have genuinely negative emotion about this harmless and incredibly fun funky pop music, there must be at least a few screws loose in your head. I'm not saying there aren't groups that do this better, but that doesn't mean this still can't be enjoyed. 12 0

Anónimo

Agosto 22, 2002

 The opening “Harder to Breathe” grooves on a funky rock foundation, with Levine hitting high tenor notes. “This Love” is just as catchy, with dialed-down guitars and an octane boost to the band’s funk engine. “She Will Be Loved” simmers down for a beachy serenade before the strolling “Sunday Morning” braids Levine’s love for Jay Kay and Stevie Wonder with some Philly-flavored blue-eyed soul. 5 1

Andrea Saint Martin

Agosto 18, 2002

 “She will be loved” hace una perfecta mezcla y sincronización de guitarra, batería y piano. Es eso lo que permite que sintamos un sentimiento agridulce cada vez que la escuchamos. Por otro lado, no se debe olvidar mencionar que la voz de Adam Levine (vocalista de la banda) es la que nos hace sentir la pena de aquel chico que ama a su mejor amiga y que está dispuesto a hacer todo por ella. 20 5
